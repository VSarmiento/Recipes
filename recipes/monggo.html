Monggo Recipe

Monggo, also known as "ginisang monggo" or "monggo guisado," is a traditional Filipino dish made from mung beans (also called "monggo" or "munggo" in the Philippines). It's a hearty, savory stew that is both nutritious and comforting, often served as a main dish with rice.

    Ingredients Needed

  • 1.5 cups Monggo balls
  • 10 pieces large shrimp
  • 1 small onion
  • 8 pieces garlic cloves
  • 1 tomato
  • 2 cups spinach
  • salt
  • black pepper
  • chicken buillon powder
  • chicken buillon cube
  • chicharon

    Preperation Steps

  1. Simmer mung bean balls in water over medium heat for approximately 45 minutes, or until the water has evaporated and the mung bean balls are tender
  2. Mince garlic
  3. Cut onions to a small size
  4. Cut tomatoes to a small size
  5. Clean spinach
  6. Peel, de-gut shrimp, and cut to desired length

    Cooking Steps

  1. Heat up oil in a large pan
  2. Add onion to level 4 heat
  3. Add garlic when onion is golden brown or clearish
  4. Add tomatoes when garlic light brown (flatten tomatoes inside pot)
  5. Once tomatoes flattened add 10 pinches of salt, 2 tbsp chicken buillon powder, and 4 tsp black pepper
  6. After 5 mins add munggo balls
  7. After 5 mins add 5 cups water (Add depending on how much soup you want)
  8. Increase the fire to high once the water is added
  9. When boiling a bit add shrimp, 1 buillon cube, mashed chicahron
  10. In a few mins taste it, if no taste add buillon powder
  11. Also add in spinach, let boil a bit
  12. Finished
